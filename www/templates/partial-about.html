Our team
Dorsey Kaufmann
Project Coordinator
Dorsey Bromwell Kaufmann is a thinker, artist, and designer who actively challenges interdisciplinary boundaries by investigating the intersections between art, design, and analytics. The core of her practice is keenly interested in social, political and environmental relationships. Through video, data visualization, installation, and animation, her transmedia projects connect the sensorial, lived experience to active global discourse. She is currently pursuing her MFA in Illustration and Design at University of Arizona and is an Information Designer for the Integrated Environmental Science and Health Risk Laboratory.

Dorsey created Ripple Effect from ideation to implementation. She was responsible for the visual language and design, sorting through the groundwater quality data, experimenting with the water cymatics installation, and generating all of the content to form a comprehensive story of Arizona's environmental quality.
Nevan Madrid
Systems Engineer
Nevan Madrid is a chemical and systems engineer. He specializes in embedded systems, and has a strong background in programming. He is interested in transdisciplinary projects that apply aspects of chemistry, data science, engineering and programming. Nevan is currently pursuing his Bachelors of Science in Chemical Engineering and Systems Engineering at University of Arizona.

Nevan became involved with Ripple Effect to help realize the installation. Working from Dorsey's vision for the experience, he used Arduino electronics platform to coordinate the audio, visual, and electrical components of the system.

Addison Kaufmann
Software Engineer
Addison Kaufmann is a software engineer and web designer. Her work is focused on applying efficient engineering and effective visual design to create engaging experiences for all users. This focus is embodied by Ripple Effect, which uses such an experience to craft a narrative around complex hydrological data. Addison has a strong background in data science, software development, and graphic arts. After completing a BS in Computer Science at University of Arizona, she currently works at Microsoft as a software engineer.

Addison built Ripple Effect’s website interface to present environmental data in an accessible format to a large audience. She was responsible for all technical decisions about the frameworks and technologies, and worked with Dorsey on the design of the user experience.
Nima Hamidi
Composer
Nima Hamidi is an Iranian composer, improviser and music scholar. His music is the sonic representation of a visual imagination, integrating music perception and computation featuring unstable timbral spectra. Influenced by a diverse musical background that includes Iranian traditional music, his compositions are a temporal study of sound that conceptualizes a cultural auditory experience. Nima is currently pursuing an MS in Computer Science. He received his PhD in music composition at the University of Iowa. Previously, he earned his MA in composition and BS in Civil Engineering.

Nima was responsible for all musical decisions. He build the software to linke the frequency of sound and the rise and fall of the data, in order to directly affect the movement patterns of the water.
