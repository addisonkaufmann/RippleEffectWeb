Biosphere 2 Installation
This installation visualizes the groundwater data collected by Arizona Department of Environmental Quality (ADEQ) on Arsenic, Lead, and Nitrate levels from over 4,000 samples in three Arizona counties over ten years.
Arizona Communities
Ripple Effect then traveled to Arizona communities that neighbor hazardous mining sites. These communities include Hayden-Winkelman, Dewey-Humboldt, Globe/Miami, and South Tucson.

During these exhibitions in the Arizona mining communities, each resident was able to plug-in their data for the water collected on their property to see how the water responds. This is made possible through UA’s data collection efforts conducted by Dr. Ramírez-Andreotta’s Integrated Environmental Science and Health Risk Laboratory.

Measuring the impact
Through surveying methods, we will evaluate and measure the impact and influence of art on the community. We are asking:
  • How does art affect people?
  • When data is incorporated into art, does it change how an individual understands the data in relation to their environment?
